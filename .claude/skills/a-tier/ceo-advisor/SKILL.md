# CEO Advisor

Strategic frameworks and tools for chief executive leadership, organizational transformation, and stakeholder management.

## Quick Start

- **Strategic Planning**: `python scripts/strategy_analyzer.py`
- **Financial Scenarios**: `python scripts/financial_scenario_analyzer.py`
- **Decision Making**: `references/executive_decision_framework.md`
- **Board Management**: `references/board_governance_investor_relations.md`
- **Culture Building**: `references/leadership_organizational_culture.md`

## Core CEO Responsibilities

### 1. Vision & Strategy

- **Vision**: Define 10-year aspirational future
- **Mission**: Clear purpose and why we exist
- **Strategy**: 3-5 year competitive positioning
- **Values**: Core beliefs and principles

**Strategic Planning Cycle**: Q1 Environmental Scan, Q2 Strategy Development, Q3 Planning & Budgeting, Q4 Communication & Launch

### 2. Capital & Resource Management

**Allocation priorities**: Core Operations (40-50%), Growth Investments (25-35%), Innovation/R&D (10-15%), Strategic Reserve (10-15%)

**Fundraising stages**: Seed/A (product-market fit), B/C (growth), Late (expansion), IPO (public), Debt (non-dilutive)

### 3. Stakeholder Leadership

**Primary**: Board, Investors, Employees, Customers
**Secondary**: Partners, Community, Media, Regulators

### 4. Organizational Leadership

**Culture Transformation**: Months 1-2 Assessment, 2-3 Design, 4-12 Implementation, 12+ Embedding
**Key Levers**: Leadership modeling, Communication, Systems alignment, Recognition, Accountability

### 5. External Representation

**Weekly**: Exec meeting, Board update, Customer/partner call, Media
**Monthly**: All-hands, Board report, Investor update
**Quarterly**: Board meeting, Earnings call, Strategy review

## Critical CEO Decisions

### Go/No-Go Decision Framework

Use `references/executive_decision_framework.md` for: M&A, Market expansion, Pivots, Large investments, Restructuring, Leadership changes

**Checklist**: Problem defined, Data gathered, Options evaluated, Stakeholders consulted, Risks assessed, Implementation planned, Success metrics defined, Communication prepared

### Crisis Management

- **Level 1** (Department): Monitor, support, review
- **Level 2** (Company): Activate crisis team, lead response, communicate frequently
- **Level 3** (Existential): Direct control, board engagement, all-hands focus, external comms

## Board Management

**Preparation**: T-4w Agenda, T-2w Materials, T-1w Distribution, T-0 Meeting

**Board Package**: CEO Letter (1-2p), Dashboard (1p), Financial review (5p), Strategic updates (10p), Risk register (2p), Appendices

**Trust Building**: Regular communication, No surprises, Transparency, Follow-through

## Investor Relations

**Earnings Cycle**: Quiet period, Release, Conference call, Follow-ups, Conferences

**Pitch Deck** (10 slides): Problem, Solution, Market, Product, Business Model, GTM, Competition, Team, Financials, Ask

## Performance Management

**Company Scorecard**: Financial (Revenue, Margin, EBITDA, Cash flow), Customer (Acquisition, Retention, NPS, LTV/CAC), Operational (Productivity, Quality, Efficiency), People (Engagement, Retention, Diversity)

**CEO Self-Assessment**: Quarterly reflection (wins, improvements, learnings, adjustments) and Annual 360 review

## Succession Planning

- **Ongoing**: Identify candidates, develop high potentials, external benchmarking
- **T-3 Years**: Formal planning, assessment, accelerated development
- **T-1 Year**: Final selection, transition planning, communication
- **Transition**: Knowledge transfer, stakeholder handoff, gradual transition

## Personal Development

**Core Competencies**: Strategic thinking, Financial acumen, Leadership presence, Communication, Decision making

**Development**: Executive coaching, Peer networking (YPO/EO), Board service, Continuous education

**Sustainability**: Protected family time, Exercise, Mental health, Vacation planning, Delegation

## Key Resources

**Books**: "Good to Great" (Collins), "Hard Thing About Hard Things" (Horowitz), "High Output Management" (Grove)
**Frameworks**: Jobs-to-be-Done, Blue Ocean Strategy, Balanced Scorecard, OKRs
**Networks**: YPO, EO, Industry associations, CEO peer groups
